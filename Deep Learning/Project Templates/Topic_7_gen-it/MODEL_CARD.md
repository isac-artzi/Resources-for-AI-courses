# Model Card — Gen-It

## Model details
- **Type:** a **variational autoencoder (VAE)** — an MLP encoder maps each 28×28
  image to a Gaussian posterior `q(z|x) = N(mu, sigma^2)` over a **2-D latent**,
  the reparameterisation trick samples `z = mu + sigma * eps`, and an MLP decoder
  (sigmoid output) reconstructs the pixels. Trained with Adam by maximising the
  ELBO (minimising reconstruction BCE + KL to `N(0, I)`) in PyTorch.
- **Size:** `latent_dim=2`, one hidden layer of `256` units in each of the encoder
  and decoder. The 2-D latent is deliberate: it makes the latent space directly
  **sliderable, walkable, and plottable** in the UI.
- **Unsupervised:** the VAE never sees the class labels during training. The known
  class of each synthetic image is used ONLY to colour the latent scatter and to
  check reconstruction error in tests.
- **Four latent-space operations:**
  1. **Generate** — decode a latent point chosen with sliders, or sampled from the
     prior `N(0, I)`.
  2. **Reconstruct** — encode an image to its posterior mean, decode it, score the
     pixel MSE.
  3. **Interpolate** — encode two images and decode a straight-line walk between
     their latents.
  4. **Latent scatter** — encode a labelled batch to see where each class lands.

## Intended use
- **Primary:** an interactive teaching demo of latent-variable generative models —
  the reparameterisation trick, the reconstruction/KL trade-off, and what a
  low-dimensional latent space *looks like* — across three clouds.
- **Out of scope:** any real image-generation task. The model is trained on
  **synthetic** shapes with a known generating rule.

## Data
- Synthetic 28×28 grayscale images from `N_CLASSES` geometric classes
  (`horizontal`, `vertical`, `diagonal`, `block`), each a simple shape plus pixel
  noise. Generated by `shared/data.py` deterministically from a seed.
- Only the generation **parameters** (`n_rows`, `noise`, `seed`) are stored in the
  Supabase `datasets` table — never the pixels; they are regenerated from the seed
  at train time.
- **No real images or personal data** are used or stored.

## Metrics
- Held-out, computed **deterministically** by decoding the posterior **mean** (no
  sampling): per-image reconstruction **BCE** (`recon_loss`), per-image **KL**, and
  the **ELBO** = `-(recon_loss + kl)`.
- Every run's metrics are persisted to the Supabase `runs` table (single source of
  truth — not recomputed in the UI).

## Privacy — what we log (and don't)
- For every served reconstruction we store the input image's **sha256 hash + shape
  + reconstruction MSE** in `image_metadata` — **never the raw pixels** (the "store
  hashes, not raw payloads" invariant).
- Fitted models (base64-pickled) live in `run_artifacts`, which is **not**
  anon-readable; only run metrics are exposed to the UI's anon key.

## Limitations
- A compact 2-D-latent VAE on a toy shape task; it will not generalize to natural
  images, and reconstructions are blurry (an expected consequence of the Gaussian
  posterior + pixel likelihood, most visible at such a small latent size).
- A 2-D latent bottleneck is chosen for *visualisability*, not fidelity: more
  latent dimensions would reconstruct better but could not be shown on a 2-D
  scatter or driven by two sliders.

## Ethical considerations
- A non-technical client must be told the model's limits plainly
  (honesty/stewardship): it is a demonstration on synthetic shapes, not a validated
  image generator. The latent scatter and interpolation build intuition for *how* a
  generative latent space is organised, but disclose the held-out metrics and the
  toy-data caveat alongside it.
